## Research Notes

### Medication Timing Facts (for content writers)
- Sileo (dexmedetomidine): FDA-approved for noise aversion; apply to gums 30-60 min before; lasts up to 3 hrs; can repeat every 2 hrs
- Trazodone: Give 90 min-2 hrs before; takes 1-3 hrs to kick in; lasts up to 12 hrs; doses spaced 8-12 hrs apart
- Gabapentin: Give ~2 hrs before; vets sometimes recommend starting as early as 8 a.m. on July 4th
- Alprazolam (Xanax): Give 30-60 min before; fast-acting; mild sedation only
- General rule: Medications must be active BEFORE panic sets in — "test dose" on a non-event evening recommended by most vets
- Combo therapy: Trazodone + Gabapentin is a common vet combo for July 4th

### French Bulldog Breed Notes (for content writers)
- Frenchies are a high-anxiety breed due to strong human-bonding (velcro dog tendency)
- Most common trigger: separation anxiety — they dislike being alone for more than a few hours
- Behavioral signs: excessive barking, destructive chewing, pacing, panting, drooling, hiding
- Cry/whine typically starts within 5-10 minutes of owner departure
- Approved meds: fluoxetine (Reconcile), clomipramine (Clomicalm) — both for separation anxiety
- "Velcro dog syndrome" angle is an untapped long-tail with strong question intent
