Key Ingredients Breakdown

Understanding what you are putting into your dog’s body is non-negotiable. Here is exactly what makes VetriScience Composure tick:

  • Thiamine (Vitamin B1): This essential vitamin plays a critical role in modulating nerve impulses. It helps support healthy brain function and is often used in veterinary medicine to promote a calm demeanor without causing drowsiness.
  • Colostrum Calming Complex (Bio-Mos®): This is the "secret sauce" of Composure. Derived from bovine colostrum, it supports the immune system and helps regulate the chemical pathways associated with stress and emotional reactivity. It works by supporting the gut-brain axis.
  • L-Theanine: An amino acid found naturally in green tea, L-Theanine promotes alpha-brain wave activity. This creates a state of "relaxed alertness," meaning your dog can stay calm without being zonked out.

This tri-fold approach makes Composure unique. It doesn't just sedate your dog; it helps regulate their emotional response to stress triggers.

Who Is VetriScience Composure Best For?

This product is an excellent tool for the average pet parent. It fits best for:

  • Puppies and Adolescents: Helps with socialization stress and fear periods.
  • Rescue Dogs: Provides a safety net while they adjust to a new environment.
  • Owners Seeking Drug-Free Options: If you are wary of prescription sedatives like Trazodone or Alprazolam, Composure offers a gentle alternative.

However, for dogs with severe separation anxiety that leads to destructive behavior (chewing doors, eliminating in the house), Composure should be used as a supplement to a comprehensive behavior modification plan, not as a standalone cure.

When to Talk to Your Veterinarian

While VetriScience Composure is safe for most dogs, you should always consult your vet before starting any new supplement, especially if your dog is currently taking:

  • Anti-anxiety medications (SSRIs like Prozac or Reconcile)
  • Sedatives
  • Thyroid medication

Additionally, if your dog’s anxiety is so severe that they are a danger to themselves or others (e.g., breaking teeth on crates, jumping through windows), do not rely solely on OTC chews. Seek professional veterinary behavior help immediately.

Important Note: Always store calming chews in a cool, dry place. The active ingredients in colostrum can degrade in high heat, reducing their efficacy.

Understanding your dog's breed-specific triggers can also make a huge difference. Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take for VetriScience Composure to work?

Most dogs show visible signs of relaxation within 30 to 60 minutes after administration. For the fastest results, give the chew on an empty stomach or use the liquid formula, which can be absorbed more quickly through the oral mucosa.

Can I give my dog VetriScience Composure every day?

Yes, it is safe for daily use. However, it is most effective for situational anxiety. If your dog needs daily support for generalized anxiety, you might find better long-term success with a product like Solliquin or a prescription medication tailored to your dog's specific biochemistry.

Is VetriScience Composure safe for puppies?

Yes, it is generally safe for puppies over 12 weeks of age. The dosage is weight-based, so weigh your puppy carefully and follow the instructions on the label. It can be particularly effective for helping puppies cope with car rides or vet visits during their critical socialization period.

What is the difference between VetriScience Composure and Composure Pro?

Composure Pro is a veterinary-exclusive formula that contains slightly higher concentrations of the key active ingredients. It is designed for dogs who need a stronger dose but are not ready for prescription medication. You can only get Composure Pro from your veterinarian's office.

Can I combine VetriScience Composure with CBD oil?

There is limited research on the interaction between CBD oil and the ingredients in Composure. While many owners do combine them for a synergistic effect, we always recommend discussing this with your holistic veterinarian first to ensure there are no underlying health risks, especially regarding liver function.
