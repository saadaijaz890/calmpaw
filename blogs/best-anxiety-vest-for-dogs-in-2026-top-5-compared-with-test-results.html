How to Choose the Right Anxiety Vest for Your Dog

Not every vest works for every dog. Here are the key factors to consider:

  • Size and fit: Measure your dog’s chest girth and length. A vest that’s too loose won’t provide enough pressure; too tight can be uncomfortable or dangerous. Follow the manufacturer’s sizing chart.
  • Material: If your dog has sensitive skin or allergies, choose a breathable, hypoallergenic fabric like cotton or spandex. Avoid neoprene if your dog tends to overheat.
  • Ease of cleaning: Dogs get dirty. Machine-washable vests are much more convenient than hand-wash-only options.
  • Type of anxiety: For noise phobias (thunder, fireworks), a full-body wrap like the Anxiety Wrap Pro may work better. For separation anxiety, the ThunderShirt is a proven choice. For car anxiety, consider the PetSafe Happy Ride.
  • Gradual introduction: No vest works instantly. Let your dog wear it for short periods while doing fun activities (treats, play) to build a positive association. Check out our step-by-step guide on introducing an anxiety vest for more tips.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take for an anxiety vest to work?

Most dogs show a noticeable calming effect within 10–20 minutes of wearing the vest. However, it’s not a magic cure—some dogs need multiple sessions to get used to the sensation. We recommend putting the vest on 15–30 minutes before a known trigger (like a storm) for best results.

Can I leave the anxiety vest on my dog all day?

No, anxiety vests are designed for short-term use during stressful events. Wearing them for more than a few hours can cause discomfort or skin irritation. Always remove the vest when your dog is calm and relaxed.

Are anxiety vests safe for puppies?

Yes, but only for puppies over 8 weeks old and under supervision. Use the smallest size available and ensure it’s not too tight. Puppies grow quickly, so you may need to size up frequently. For more on puppy anxiety, see our puppy anxiety training guide.

Do anxiety vests work for separation anxiety?

They can help, but they’re not a standalone solution. The pressure can reduce the initial panic, but separation anxiety often requires behavior modification, crate training, or professional help. Combine the vest with our separation anxiety guide for best results.

How do I clean an anxiety vest?

Check the care label. Most vests can be machine washed on a gentle cycle and air dried. Avoid fabric softeners, which can reduce the pressure effect. For hand-wash-only vests, use mild soap and cold water, then hang to dry.
