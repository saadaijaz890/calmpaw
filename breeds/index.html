Dog Anxiety by Breed

Every breed has different anxiety triggers, temperaments, and responses to calming products. Find your breed for a tailored guide.

All Breed Guides

🐕High Energy

Australian Shepherd

Velcro dogs with intense separation anxiety. Needs mental stimulation above all.

 🐶Vocal

Beagle

Howling and apartment anxiety specialists. Scent-driven calming works well.

 🐾Working Breed

Border Collie

Hyper-intelligent and easily understimulated. Anxiety from boredom is common.

 👑Companion Breed

Cavalier King Charles

Bred to never be alone. Separation anxiety is nearly universal in this breed.

 🐕‍🦺Nervous Breed

Chihuahua

Big anxiety in a small body. Fear-based reactivity and noise sensitivity are common.

 🦊Herding

Corgi

Herding instinct drives anxiety. Vocal anxiety and clingy behavior when bored.

 🌭Stubborn

Dachshund

Separation anxiety and storm phobia. IVDD risk makes calming especially important.

 🛡️Velcro Dog

Doberman

Intensely bonded to one person. Struggles with absence more than most breeds.

 🥖Brachycephalic

French Bulldog

Breathing anxiety + separation anxiety combo. Needs special heat management.

 🐺Working Breed

German Shepherd

Fearless on the job, anxious at home. Hypervigilance and noise sensitivity are common.

 🌟Social Breed

Golden Retriever

Friendly but anxious alone. Senior Goldens are prone to nighttime anxiety.

 ❄️Escape Artist

Husky

Howls and escapes when anxious. Apartment anxiety is a major challenge.

 🍫High Energy

Labrador Retriever

Destructive separation anxiety when under-exercised. One of the most anxious breeds.

 💪Misunderstood

Pitbull

People-oriented and prone to separation distress. Responds well to structure.

 🎭Sensitive

Poodle

Highly intelligent and emotionally sensitive. Grooming anxiety is very common.

 😤Brachycephalic

Pug

Anxiety amplified by breathing issues. Overheating risk during anxious episodes.

 🩶Velcro Dog

Weimaraner

The ultimate velcro dog. Severe separation anxiety is nearly guaranteed without training.

 🎀Feisty

Yorkshire Terrier

Bold personality masks real anxiety. Noise sensitivity and barking anxiety are common.
