Dog Separation Anxiety After Returning to Work

Millions of dogs developed separation anxiety when owners started working from home — then panicked when they went back. Here's the 4-week protocol to fix it.

🔬 Vet-reviewed📅 Updated 2026⌛ 8 min read

Why This Happened

Dogs that spent months with their owners constantly present recalibrated their baseline to "human always here." When that changed abruptly, their nervous system treated normal absence as a genuine threat. This isn't bad behavior — it's a learned expectation being violated. The fix requires systematically rebuilding a new expectation: "owner leaves, owner always comes back, everything is fine."

Key insight: The goal isn't to teach your dog to tolerate being alone. It's to teach them that alone time is safe and predictable. These are different goals with different training approaches.

Assess Your Dog's Current Level

🟡

Mild (manageable)

Whines briefly, settles within 20 min, no destruction. Can be addressed with products + gradual absences.

🔴

Moderate (needs work)

Barks/howls 30+ min, some destruction near exits, may not eat while alone. Needs the full 4-week protocol.

🟢

Severe (vet first)

Self-injury, extreme destruction, refuses food for 12+ hours, escaping. Start with vet consultation before the training protocol.

The 4-Week Protocol

Week 1: Desensitize Departure Cues

Your departure routine — keys, shoes, bag, jacket — triggers anxiety before you even leave. Break the association by performing these cues repeatedly without leaving.

1

Pick up keys, set them down. Repeat 20x/day.

Do this while watching TV, working, making coffee. Keys become meaningless.

2

Put on shoes, sit back down. 20x/day.

Shoes stop predicting your departure. Your dog stops escalating when they appear.

3

Put on jacket, walk to door, come back in. 10x/day.

The door is not a one-way valve. This is the most important desensitization step.

Week 2: Build Absences from 10 Seconds

Start leaving for genuinely short times — shorter than your dog starts to react. Step outside for 5 seconds, come back calmly. Gradually increase to 1 minute, 5 minutes, 15 minutes. Never return when your dog is actively distressed — wait for any pause in barking before re-entering.

Week 3: Bridge to Real-World Absences

Work up to 1–2 hour absences. Introduce a food-stuffed Kong every time you leave (crate or free-roaming) — it creates a positive association and occupies the dog during the highest-anxiety first 20 minutes. Set up a camera (Furbo, Wyze) to monitor reactions remotely.

Week 4: Full Work Schedule

Build to your full absence duration. Most dogs that have completed weeks 1–3 can handle full workdays by week 4. Maintain a consistent departure routine — same time, same sequence, minimal fuss. Long emotional goodbyes increase anxiety.

What NOT to Do

  • Don't get another dog immediately — a second dog doesn't fix separation anxiety and creates two anxious dogs
  • Don't punish anxious behavior — it's not disobedience, it's distress
  • Don't make departures emotional — long goodbyes increase anticipatory anxiety
  • Don't skip the desensitization step — jumping straight to full-day absences usually makes things worse

FAQs

How long does it take to fix post-lockdown separation anxiety?

Mild cases: 2–4 weeks with consistent protocol. Moderate cases: 4–8 weeks. Severe cases: 3–6 months, often requiring veterinary medication alongside the behavioral protocol.

Should I hire a dog walker to break up the day?

Yes, for dogs that can't yet handle full-day absences. A midday visit reduces total alone time significantly and breaks the anxiety cycle. It's a management strategy, not a cure — continue the protocol alongside it.

My dog was fine before working from home — will they go back to normal?

Most do, with the protocol above. Dogs that were previously comfortable alone usually respond faster than dogs with no prior experience of being alone. The baseline is easier to rebuild than to create from scratch.
