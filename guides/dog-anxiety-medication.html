Dog Anxiety Medication: Every Option Explained

Prescription or OTC? Short-term or daily? Here's a vet-reviewed breakdown of every dog anxiety medication — what it does, when to use it, and what to avoid.

🔬 Vet-reviewed📅 Updated 2026⌛ 10 min read

Do Dogs Really Need Medication for Anxiety?

Not always — but for moderate to severe anxiety, medication is often the difference between a dog that can learn and one that can't. Anxiety keeps the brain in fight-or-flight mode. No amount of training reaches a dog in that state. The right medication lowers the baseline so behavioral work can actually stick.

Key rule: Medication is a tool, not a cure. It works best combined with behavior modification, environmental management, and calming products.

Prescription Medications (Vet Required)

Fluoxetine (Prozac / Reconcile)

The most commonly prescribed daily anxiety medication for dogs. FDA-approved as Reconcile for separation anxiety. Takes 4–8 weeks to reach full effect. Best for generalized anxiety, separation anxiety, and compulsive behaviors.

Dose: 1–2 mg/kg daily. Side effects: mild sedation and appetite changes in the first 2 weeks — usually resolve.

Trazodone

Situational sedative used for vet visits, travel, fireworks, and post-surgery recovery. Works within 1–2 hours. Often combined with fluoxetine for dogs on daily treatment who still need event-based relief.

Important: Trazodone can cause serotonin syndrome if combined with certain supplements. Never give with St. John's Wort or MAOIs.

Alprazolam (Xanax)

Fast-acting benzodiazepine for severe acute anxiety — thunderstorms, fireworks, travel. Onset 30–60 minutes, duration 4–6 hours. Controlled substance; risk of dependence with frequent use. Reserve for truly panic-level events.

Gabapentin

Originally an anticonvulsant, now widely used off-label for anxiety and pain. Very safe with minimal side effects. Good choice for senior dogs or those with both anxiety and pain. Onset 1–2 hours.

Clomipramine (Clomicalm)

FDA-approved tricyclic antidepressant for separation anxiety. Slower onset than fluoxetine (4–8 weeks) but effective for compulsive behaviors. Less commonly prescribed today since fluoxetine has a better side-effect profile.

Over-the-Counter Options

🍖

Calming Chews

L-theanine, melatonin, chamomile blends. Best for mild anxiety. VetriScience Composure is the top pick for onset speed.

🌿

Zylkene (Alpha-casozepine)

Milk protein derivative with benzodiazepine-like effects. Very safe, no sedation. Daily supplement or 1–2 days before events.

💊

Melatonin

0.1 mg/kg up to 3 mg. Good for noise phobia and nighttime anxiety. Avoid in dogs with hormonal conditions. Give 30 min before trigger.

🐄

Purina Calming Care

Probiotic (BL999 strain). Takes 6 weeks to build up but produces measurable baseline anxiety reduction in clinical trials.

CBD for Dog Anxiety

CBD (cannabidiol) is widely marketed for dog anxiety. The evidence is mixed — a 2021 Cornell study found CBD reduced cortisol and anxiety scores during car travel. However, quality varies enormously across brands. If you try CBD, use a product with a third-party COA (certificate of analysis), 0% THC, and dosed at 1–2 mg/kg.

Best CBD products: ElleVet Sciences and Honest Paws are the most studied. Avoid anything without lab testing documentation.

When to See a Vet

If your dog's anxiety causes self-injury, prevents normal daily function, or hasn't improved after 4–6 weeks of OTC products and behavioral work, book a vet appointment. Ask specifically about a behavioral consultation — a veterinary behaviorist can create a full medication + training protocol tailored to your dog.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take for dog anxiety medication to work?

Daily medications like fluoxetine take 4–8 weeks for full effect. Situational drugs like trazodone work within 1–2 hours. OTC calming chews take 30–60 minutes.

Can I give my dog human anxiety medication?

No. Human medications like Xanax or Valium are dosed very differently for dogs and can be toxic in the wrong amount. Always use veterinary-prescribed doses.

Are calming chews as effective as prescription medication?

For mild anxiety, yes. For moderate to severe anxiety, prescription medication is significantly more effective. Calming chews work best as a complement to other interventions.

Is it safe to give my dog melatonin?

Generally yes at 0.1 mg/kg (max 3 mg). Avoid melatonin in dogs with insulin resistance, Cushing's disease, or those on immunosuppressants. Always check the label — some human melatonin contains xylitol which is toxic to dogs.
