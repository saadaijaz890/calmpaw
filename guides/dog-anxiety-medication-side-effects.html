Dog Anxiety Medication Side Effects: What to Expect

Starting your dog on anxiety medication? Here's exactly what side effects to expect from every common drug, which are normal, and which need a vet call.

🔬 Vet-reviewed📅 Updated 2026⌛ 8 min read

Why Side Effects Happen

Anxiety medications work by modifying neurotransmitter levels or receptor activity — processes that affect the whole brain and body, not just anxiety pathways. Most side effects are dose-dependent and temporary, appearing in the first 1–2 weeks as the body adjusts. Knowing what's normal prevents unnecessary panic and helps you catch genuinely concerning reactions early.

General rule: Mild side effects in the first 2 weeks are usually normal adjustment. Severe, worsening, or persistent side effects beyond 3 weeks always warrant a vet call.

Fluoxetine (Prozac / Reconcile)

💦

Common (expect these)

Reduced appetite weeks 1–2, mild lethargy, slight behavior changes during adjustment. Usually resolve by week 3.

⚠️

Call your vet if

Not eating for 3+ days, vomiting, significant agitation or aggression, tremors, or seizures.

Fluoxetine takes 4–8 weeks to reach full effect. Many owners see a slight worsening of anxiety or irritability in weeks 1–3 before improvement — this is a known adjustment phase, not a sign the drug isn't working.

Trazodone

💦

Common (expect these)

Sedation (the intended effect at higher doses), mild stumbling, dilated pupils. Usually proportional to dose.

⚠️

Call your vet if

Excessive sedation where dog can't walk, vomiting, agitation (paradoxical reaction), or signs of priapism (rare).

Serotonin syndrome risk: Trazodone combined with other serotonergic drugs (MAOIs, St. John's Wort, some pain medications) can cause serotonin syndrome — tremors, hyperthermia, seizures. Always tell your vet every supplement your dog takes.

Alprazolam (Xanax)

💦

Common (expect these)

Sedation, coordination issues (ataxia), increased appetite. Dose-dependent — effects are stronger at higher doses.

⚠️

Call your vet if

Paradoxical excitement/aggression (occurs in some dogs), severe ataxia, respiratory depression, or collapse.

Benzodiazepines like alprazolam cause physical dependence with regular use. Never stop abruptly after daily use of 4+ weeks — taper under vet guidance to prevent withdrawal seizures.

Gabapentin

💦

Common (expect these)

Sedation, wobbly gait especially in first few days, mild increased appetite. One of the safest profiles of any anxiety medication.

⚠️

Call your vet if

Severe sedation, loss of balance, sudden behavioral changes, or symptoms don't improve after 1 week of adjustment.

Clomipramine (Clomicalm)

💦

Common (expect these)

Sedation, dry mouth (increased water intake), constipation, mild appetite changes in weeks 1–2.

⚠️

Call your vet if

Seizures, severe vomiting, heart rhythm changes (rare), urinary retention, or jaundice (yellowing of eyes/gums).

Sileo (Dexmedetomidine)

FDA-approved specifically for noise aversion. Applied as an oral gel to the gum. Side effects include mild sedation, pale gums (temporary), and low heart rate. Rarely causes vomiting. One of the cleanest side-effect profiles for situational anxiety.

When to Go to the Emergency Vet

Emergency symptoms: Seizures, collapse, difficulty breathing, blue or white gums, body temperature over 104°F (hyperthermia), extreme agitation or self-injury, or sudden severe behavioral changes. These require immediate veterinary care regardless of which medication your dog is on.

FAQs

My dog seems more anxious after starting fluoxetine — is this normal?

Yes, in the first 1–3 weeks. SSRIs can briefly increase anxiety during the adjustment period before they start reducing it. This is why vets sometimes prescribe a short course of trazodone to bridge the first 2 weeks. If worsening persists beyond 3 weeks, contact your vet.

Can anxiety medications affect my dog's personality long-term?

Properly dosed medications should reduce anxiety without blunting personality. If your dog seems like a "zombie" — excessively sedated, unresponsive, or disengaged — the dose is likely too high. This is reversible with dose adjustment.

How long does my dog need to stay on anxiety medication?

Situational drugs (trazodone, alprazolam): only as needed. Daily medications (fluoxetine): typically 6–12 months minimum; some dogs stay on them lifelong. Many dogs can be tapered off daily medications after 12–18 months with concurrent behavior modification.
