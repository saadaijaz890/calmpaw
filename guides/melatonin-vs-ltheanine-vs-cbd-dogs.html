Melatonin vs L-Theanine vs CBD for Dog Anxiety

Three popular natural supplements, very different mechanisms. Here's exactly how melatonin, L-theanine, and CBD compare — what each does best, dosing, and when to combine them.

🔬 Vet-reviewed📅 Updated 2026⌛ 8 min read

Why This Comparison Matters

These are the three most purchased dog anxiety supplements — and they work through completely different mechanisms. Choosing the right one (or combination) depends on your dog's anxiety type, timing needs, and your budget. Using the wrong one wastes money and leaves your dog under-supported.

Melatonin: Best For

Nighttime restlessness, noise phobia (fireworks, thunderstorms), and post-surgery anxiety. The mild sedative effect is an advantage for nighttime use and an event you want the dog to sleep through. Give 30 minutes before the trigger.

Critical check: Always verify melatonin products contain no xylitol — a common sweetener in human supplements that is highly toxic to dogs. Plain melatonin only.

L-Theanine: Best For

Everyday anxiety, vet visits, training sessions, travel, and any situation where you need calm without sedation. L-theanine is unique in producing an "alert calm" — the dog is relaxed but still engaged and able to learn. This makes it ideal for training anxious dogs.

Combination Strategies

Storm or fireworks

Melatonin (sedation) + L-theanine (GABA) = best OTC combo for acute noise events. Give both 45 min before.

🐙

FAQs

Which is strongest: melatonin, L-theanine, or CBD?

In order of anxiolytic strength for most dogs: CBD ≥ L-theanine > melatonin. But "strongest" isn't always best — the right choice depends on what type of anxiety relief you need and whether sedation is acceptable.

Can I give all three together?

Yes — there are no known interactions between melatonin, L-theanine, and CBD. The combination is commonly used for severe event-based anxiety (fireworks, thunderstorms) when OTC options are all that's available.

Why isn't my dog responding to L-theanine?

Most commonly: dose too low, given too late (needs 30–45 min minimum), or anxiety is too severe for OTC supplementation alone. L-theanine works best for mild to moderate anxiety. Severe anxiety typically needs prescription medication.
